Key findings

57M

People living with dementia globally in 2019 — a 160% increase in prevalence since 1990 (GBD 2021)

153M

Projected cases by 2050 — nearly tripling current numbers as global populations age

60%

Of all dementia cases occur in low- and middle-income countries, where healthcare access is most limited

53%

Of all people living with dementia are women — biological and longevity factors both contribute to this disparity

Why this matters

Alzheimer's and related dementias represent one of the fastest-growing neurological crises globally. Unlike cancer or heart disease, dementia currently has no disease-modifying cure — existing drugs only slow progression. The steep projected rise to 153 million cases by 2050 is driven almost entirely by population aging and growth, not changes in individual risk. This makes early detection research, caregiver infrastructure, and policy investment urgent priorities, especially in lower-income regions where most future cases will occur.
