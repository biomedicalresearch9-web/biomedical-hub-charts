Key findings

+62 pts

Rise in DTP3 coverage from 1980 to 2019 — the longest sustained immunization campaign in history

−5 pts

Drop in global coverage during 2020–21 COVID-19 disruption — the largest single-year decline in 30 years

25M

Children left under- or unvaccinated in 2021, the highest number since 2005 (WHO, 2022)

83%

Global measles coverage in 2023 — still below the 95% threshold needed for herd immunity

Why this matters

Vaccines are among the most cost-effective public health tools ever developed. The dip seen in 2020–2021 illustrates how fragile immunization infrastructure can be during global crises — and why sustained investment in health systems matters even between outbreaks. Measles, which requires 95% population immunity to prevent outbreaks, remains below that threshold globally, making vaccination equity an active immunology challenge, not a solved one.
