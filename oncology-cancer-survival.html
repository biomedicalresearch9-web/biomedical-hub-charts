Why this matters

The dramatic improvements in survival across most cancers since 1975 reflect the compounding impact of early detection, targeted therapies, and decades of clinical research. Lung cancer's persistently low survival — despite modest recent gains — underscores why it remains one of oncology's most urgent frontiers. The arrival of immunotherapy (checkpoint inhibitors) since 2015 is already shifting lung cancer outcomes and will likely appear more dramatically in the next decade of SEER data.
