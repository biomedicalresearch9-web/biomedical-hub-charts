Why this matters

The 70-year decline in cardiovascular mortality is one of the most significant achievements in medical history, driven by a combination of public health policy (smoking bans), pharmacology (statins, beta-blockers), and surgical innovation (bypass surgery, stents). The COVID-19 era reversal — the largest single increase in decades — shows how fragile these gains can be. Despite all progress, heart disease remains the #1 cause of death in the US, underscoring why cardiovascular research and preventive care remain critical.
