Key findings

−65%

Decline in new HIV infections since the 1995 peak of 3.5 million — driven by prevention programs and treatment access (UNAIDS 2024)

32M

People on antiretroviral therapy globally by end of 2023, up from 7.7 million in 2010 — a historic scale-up

−74%

Decline in AIDS-related deaths since the 2004 peak — ART transformed HIV from a death sentence to a manageable condition

40.8M

People living with HIV globally in 2024 — total remains high because ART extends lives, not because infections are rising

Why this matters

The HIV/AIDS epidemic is one of epidemiology's most complex case studies — showing both how quickly an infectious disease can spread globally and how sustained international cooperation, scientific breakthroughs (antiretroviral therapy), and funding (PEPFAR, Global Fund) can bend a pandemic's curve. Despite this progress, 1.3 million new infections per year means the epidemic is far from over, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa where 63% of new infections occur among women and girls. The gap between treatment access in high-income versus low-income countries remains a central equity challenge in global health.
